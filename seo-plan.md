## 1-page content blueprint (NoviaChat “AI chat authority” in Spanish)

### Goal (what success looks like)

Create a Spanish-first content hub that:

1. ranks for high-intent “chat de IA” comparison queries (Mexico / España / Argentina),
2. earns trust via transparent testing,
3. converts readers into NoviaChat trials **without** feeling like a promo listicle.

### Positioning (how you win)

**“Comparativas y guías de chats de IA en español con pruebas reales + enfoque LATAM/España + privacidad y seguridad.”**
You’re not trying to cover “all AI.” You’re trying to own “AI chat in Spanish” (and especially companionship-style chat) with credibility.

### Core architecture (2 pillars + clusters)

**Pillar A (flagship, broad):**
**“Mejores chats de IA en español (Actualizado 2026): comparativa con pruebas reales”**

* Evergreen URL (no year in slug); update title/H1 yearly.
* Purpose: capture broad “best AI chat” traffic and funnel to sub-guides.

**Pillar B (your moat, still SFW):**
**“Mejores chats de compañía con IA en español (2026): guía y comparativa”**

* Focus on conversation quality, safety, privacy, boundaries, and Spanish fluency.

**Clusters:** Narrow pages that target long-tail intent and feed the pillars via internal links:

* “gratis”, “alternativas a ChatGPT”, “con memoria”, “privacidad”, “para estudiar”, “para trabajo”, “para practicar conversación”, “para compañía”, etc.

### URL and publishing rules (simple, no coding assumptions)

* Publish as `/guias/<slug>` (fits your existing structure).
* **Slug = evergreen**, e.g. `/guias/mejores-chats-ia-en-espanol`
  Title/H1 can include “2026”.
* Every cluster post must link to:

    * its relevant pillar (near the top + again near the bottom),
    * 1–2 “money” pages (e.g., `/novia-ia`, `/novia-virtual`, `/novia-virtual-gratis`, `/planes`) **only when contextually relevant**.

### “Trust moat” methodology (your repeatable original research)

Create a **fixed Spanish test protocol** you reuse across all tools (and publish it on the pillar page):

**10 test prompts (Spanish) + 1–5 scoring** on:

* Spanish naturalness (neutral + MX/ES/AR notes)
* coherence in a long conversation
* usefulness (actionable answers)
* safety controls / refusals quality
* privacy clarity (account, deletion, data export, etc.)
* memory/context handling (if applicable)
* multimodal support (if relevant)

**Output format:**

* A comparison table (8–12 tools max)
* “Best for…” winners (Best free, Best Spanish, Best privacy, Best studying, Best companionship, etc.)
* Short “tool cards” with pros/cons + who it’s for + what to avoid.

### How to include NoviaChat without looking spammy

Use a consistent disclosure + honest positioning:

**Disclosure block (copy/paste):**

> “Transparencia: Somos el equipo de NoviaChat. Esta comparativa usa prompts iguales y criterios públicos. Incluimos pros y contras de todas las opciones, incluida la nuestra.”

**Placement rule:**

* NoviaChat appears as **one option** in relevant “Best for…” sections (especially companionship + Spanish quality).
* Avoid “NoviaChat is #1 for everything.” That kills credibility.

### Content templates (copy/paste outlines)

**Pillar template (must-have sections)**

1. TL;DR winners (top 3)
2. Comparison table
3. Methodology (prompts + scoring)
4. Winners by intent (“mejor para…”)
5. Tool cards (8–12)
6. FAQ (PAA-style questions)
7. Update log (“Last updated” + 3 bullets of changes)

**Cluster template**

1. Short answer (2–4 sentences)
2. What to choose if you want X (clear recommendations)
3. Mini table (top 3–7)
4. Link to pillar (“ver comparativa completa”)
5. FAQ
6. CTA block (only if relevant)

### Freshness system (daily work without daily thin posts)

Instead of “1 new post/day forever,” do this weekly:

* **2x/week:** publish a new cluster post
* **2x/week:** update a pillar/cluster (pricing, features, add/remove tools, improve table, add 1–2 FAQs)
* **2x/week:** publish a short support piece (definitions, checklists, privacy explainers)
* **1x/week:** internal linking + “what changed” logs + improve intros

### Safety + reach guardrails (important for your category)

* Keep the blog examples **PG‑13/SFW** to maximize distribution and avoid accidental SafeSearch throttling of the whole content section.
* Separate “privacy/safety/boundaries” guidance from anything suggestive.
* Avoid medical/therapy positioning (“not a therapist”) and focus on companionship/conversation.
