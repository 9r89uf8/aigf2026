## A simple “test protocol” that becomes your moat

You don’t need a lab. You need consistency.

Create 10 prompts in Spanish and use them on every tool:

Examples (safe/SFW):

* “Explícame X como si tuviera 12 años.”
* “Hazme un plan de estudio para 7 días sobre X.”
* “Resume este texto y dime 3 ideas clave.”
* “Ayúdame a escribir un mensaje amable para decir ‘no’ sin sonar grosero.”
* “Mantén el contexto de lo que te dije antes: [A], ahora quiero [B].”
* “Dame 5 preguntas para conocer mejor a alguien (tono respetuoso).”
* “Simula una conversación corta donde tú eres… (amistoso, no romántico explícito).”
* “Detecta contradicciones en este párrafo…”
* “Dame opciones y luego recomiéndame 1 con razones.”
* “¿Qué datos guardas y cómo puedo borrarlos?” (si aplica)

Score each tool 1–5 on:

* naturalidad del español
* coherencia en conversación larga
* utilidad
* seguridad/controles
* claridad de privacidad

That’s “original research” in the sense Google means for reviews: you’re adding insight beyond copying feature lists. ([Google for Developers][4])

## “Daily blogging” without triggering thin-content risk

Instead of “1 new post every day,” do “daily work” with fewer, stronger publishes.

A cadence that’s safer and usually performs better:

* **2 days/week:** publish 1 strong cluster post
* **2 days/week:** improve an existing page (update table, add 1 new tool, add FAQ, tighten intro)
* **2 days/week:** write a short supporting page that answers 1 question (but only if it truly adds value)
* **1 day/week:** internal linking + distribution + outreach

This keeps you productive daily, but avoids the pattern Google calls out: lots of pages created mainly to manipulate rankings. ([Google for Developers][2])

## One thing you must plan for: SafeSearch and “adult-adjacent” risk

Because NoviaChat is “AI girlfriend / companion,” you’re adjacent to a category Google often treats cautiously.

If you ever have **explicit content** (or even “suggestive enough” content), Google recommends grouping explicit pages on a **separate domain or subdomain**. ([Google for Developers][9])
And Google supports a `meta name="rating" content="adult"` signal for adult pages. ([Google for Developers][10])

Even if you keep everything PG‑13, it’s worth planning:

* keep the SEO blog’s tone and examples non-explicit
* avoid explicit thumbnails/media on indexable pages
* be careful with how pages link to anything explicit (SafeSearch can be sensitive) ([Google for Developers][11])

This is less about morality and more about not accidentally throttling your entire blog’s reach.
